About

I’m Julia, an Applied Machine Learning student at UAL’s Creative Computing Institute (CCI), with a background in Product Design at Central Saint Martins. My practice sits at the intersection of design, computation, and emerging AI systems, with a focus on how technology can meaningfully shape creative processes and experiences. I’m particularly interested in generative systems, human–AI interaction, and the development of tools that expand how we design, think, and create across digital and physical environments. I approach my work through both a design and technical lens, combining coding, prototyping, and research-led experimentation. My background in product design at CSM has shaped a strong foundation in human-centred thinking, material exploration, and conceptual development, which I now translate into computational and machine learning contexts. I’m drawn to projects that explore new creative workflows and challenge conventional boundaries between disciplines. Alongside my studies, I enjoy building, experimenting with new technologies, and exploring ideas through hands-on making and iterative design.
